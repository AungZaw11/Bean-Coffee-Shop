Coffee Tasting Session

Coffee tasting, or cupping, is the practice of evaluating the flavors, aromas, and characteristics of coffee. It's used by professionals and enthusiasts to assess coffee quality or explore its unique profiles. Here’s an overview:

Key Steps in Coffee Tasting:

  • Smelling the Grounds: Assess the aroma of freshly ground coffee.
  • Breaking the Crust: After brewing, break the layer of coffee grounds floating on top and smell the released aroma.
  • Tasting: Slurp the coffee (to aerate it), letting it spread across your palate.

Brewing Classes

Brewing classes are interactive workshops designed to teach coffee lovers and professionals how to craft the perfect cup using various methods. These sessions cater to all skill levels and focus on mastering techniques, understanding equipment, and appreciating coffee's unique characteristics.

What You’ll Learn in Brewing Classes:

  • Brewing Methods: Hands-on practice with pour-over, French press, AeroPress, espresso, siphon, and moka pot techniques.
  • Insights: Learn about bean origins, roast profiles, grind sizes, and water quality.
  • Equipment Usage: Proper use and maintenance of tools like grinders, scales, and kettles.
  • Techniques: Master ideal brew ratios, water temperatures, and extraction times.
  • Tasting Skills: Develop your palate to identify flavor notes, acidity, body, and aftertaste.
  • Latte Art (Optional): Advanced courses may include barista techniques and latte art.

Who Should Attend?

  • Home coffee enthusiasts aiming to elevate their brewing skills.
  • Aspiring baristas preparing for professional opportunities.
  • Anyone curious about exploring the world of specialty coffee.
